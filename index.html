Breaking down how I align my timeframes to get the best entries.

When you understand timeframe alignment, you simply put the odd of a successful trade in your favour. One thing we must understand is that price is fractal. This means whatever is happening on the higher timeframe is also happening on the lower timeframe. In other words the lower timeframe delivers price to where the higher timeframe is intended. When aligning timeframes, first things first we must have a higher timeframe, this is where we form our daily bias. Next is our medium timeframe, this is where we position ourselves for a setup. And lastly is our lower timeframe, this is where we take our entries. I use the 4H timeframe as my higher timeframe to build my bias. The 1H timeframe as my medium timeframe. The 15M timeframe as my entry timeframe. Now, when I open my chart for the morning, the first thing I do is identify the current trend of the market on the 4H timeframe. Next thing I do is observe key leves in form of FVG price is respecting / disrespecting. Example: If I have a bullish trend but price has disrespected a bullish FVG with strong displacement, I would be looking to sell for that day and vice versa. Now that I have a sell bias, next is to go to the 1H timeframe and mark out my current range because price is fractal, I would want to see price respect a sell keylevel on the 1H timeframe. Now that I have aligned my medium timeframe to my higher timeframe bias, next thing I would do is to go to the 15M timeframe and mark out lows/highs. Because I have a selI trade bias, I would want to see Asian high get taken out at London open. To reflect, we have a clear bias, our medium timeframe is aligned with our higher timeframe, price has manipulated Asian high. Now the next thing I want to see is what I call an intentional CISD, what this means is that, the candle(s) that leads to the CISD must be displacement candles. I enter at the CISD and target an IRL. This is how I align my timeframs for an A+ setup.

Understanding Market Structure

A simple explanation of how price moves and why it matters.

Market structure is the foundation of trading. If you understand market structure then you have solved up to 50% of the trading puzzle. Market structure is the basically the movement of price. Price moves in three major ways: Up, down and sideways. The upward movement of price is called the UPTREND. When the market is in an Uptrend, price is making a series of higher highs and higher lows. In this trend, our major focus is mainly to buy. The downward movement of price is called the DOWNTREND. When the market is in a downtrend, it means proce is making a series of lower highs and lower lows. In this trend, our major focus is to sell. The side ways movement of price is CONSOLIDATION. Here price is just moving up and down without breaking a major level. When price is consolidating, its a signal to pause and just observe. A major take away here is that, ideally we want to trade with the trend. Buy when the market is buying, sell when the market is selling and stay away when the market is consolidating.
